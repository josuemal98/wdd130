History

Rafting on the Cangrejal River began in the 1990s and early 2000s, when eco lodges and local tour companies first explored the river’s steep drops and big boulders. As adventure tourism grew, they developed guided rafting trips, and today the Cangrejal is known as one of the top white water destinations in Central America.

As rafting became more established, the Cangrejal River Valley grew into a small adventure hub, attracting travelers from around the world. New lodges, certified guides, and improved access from La Ceiba helped the sport expand, turning the river into a year‑round destination for both beginners and thrill‑seekers. Today, rafting is one of the main reasons visitors come to the Cangrejal, combining excitement, nature, and local hospitality in one unforgettable experience
